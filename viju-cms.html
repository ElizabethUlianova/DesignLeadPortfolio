Senior Product Designer

Designing the content management system of the streaming service from scratch.

Main tasks ✔

Internal tooling design

Designed the full CMS used by editorial, content ops, and marketing teams — covering content ingestion, metadata management, catalogue curation, and homepage scheduling.

Complex workflow design

Mapped and simplified complex multi-step workflows for publishing content across platforms — reducing the time-to-publish from days to hours.

User research with internal users

Ran discovery sessions with editorial and content ops teams to understand pain points in existing tools and prioritise what to build first.

Key results 🎁

Reduced publishing time

Content publishing time reduced by 60%. Editorial teams could schedule and update catalogue content without engineering support.

High adoption

100% adoption across content teams within 2 weeks of launch. The design prioritised familiarity and progressive disclosure to reduce onboarding friction.

Fewer support requests

Support tickets from internal teams dropped by 40% in the first month — clear UI and contextual help reduced errors and confusion.

Process

Internal tools are often the neglected half of a product — but they directly determine how fast the user-facing product can move. I approached this CMS as a product in its own right, starting with a thorough audit of the existing tooling (a patchwork of spreadsheets, a legacy system, and Slack threads).

The key insight from research was that different teams had very different mental models of the same content — editorial thought in stories, ops thought in assets, marketing thought in campaigns. The final design had to reconcile all three views without forcing anyone to think in an unfamiliar way.
